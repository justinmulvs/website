I help Founders and Leaders become more Conscious, Connected, and Effective.

Leadership can be lonely. As a Leader, you constantly expose yourself to the demands of others: your investors, your customers, your employees, the market. Sometimes it can be hard to tell up from down.

As a leader, you might recognize some of these challenges:

  • You struggle to stop working “in” the business and start working “on” it.
  • You feel like your team (including your senior leaders) isn't as effective as it could be. You’re aware that you somehow get in the way, but unclear how.
  • You get overwhelmed and struggle to identify what's most important. You get trapped in procrastination and avoidance. This drives a sense of impostor syndrome—a belief that you’re not “really” a leader, that evenutally you'll be "found out".
  • You feel burned out and unfulfilled. Something is off, but you can’t determine what.
  • You feel a pervasive sense of loneliness. You believe that no one around you “gets it”.
  • You struggle with fear, uncertainty, and doubt. You're unsure if it's normal, what to do with it, or how to process it.
  • You struggle to find time to work on yourself.
  • You desire a partner to give you feedback and help optimize your leadership. You're high performing, but you know you can be better.

Sound familiar? If so, let's connect. I might be able to help.

About Me

My Mission: I help Leaders and Leadership teams become more conscious, connected, and effective through conscious coaching and facilitation.

After receiving an undergraduate degree in Economics and Mathematics from Binghamton University, I spent the first inning of my work life at early stage technology companies. My career kicked off as employee #3 at a fast-growing tech startup. From there, I ran data for a $9M fundraise, led the product and data side of an acquisition, and supported C-level leaders in every department of the modern tech org (including CEOs, CTOs, CROs, CFOs, CPOs, and COOs, but who’s counting?).

It was early on in that journey that I knew I wanted to be a coach. As I developed myself professionally, I fell in love with psychology. I learned that I have a unique ability to see the many ways that leader’s personal dysfunction leaks out into their teams and companies, something that I call Founder DNA. I quickly realized that if I could help leaders see all of the ways they get in the way of themselves and their team, it would create more value than all of my other skills combined. Leadership is leverage, after all.

Now, nearly a decade after that initial insight, I’ve worked with 75+ leaders over 1000’s of 1-on-1 sessions, facilitated masterminds, and experiential workshops to do exactly that. The leaders I work with report being able to more adeptly understand and work with their emotions, communicate, and move things forward, as well as having deeper relationships and more satisfaction in their personal and professional lives.

If you’re interested, please do reach out. It would be a delight to meet and work with you.
